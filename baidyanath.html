Baidyanath Dham, Deoghar

Location

Baidyanath Dham is located in Deoghar district of Jharkhand.

About & History

Baidyanath Dham, also known as Baba Dham, is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India and attracts millions of devotees every year. According to Hindu mythology, the temple is associated with the story of Ravana, who worshipped Lord Shiva to gain divine blessings. It is believed that Ravana offered his heads to Lord Shiva, and pleased by his devotion, Shiva appeared and healed him. Because Shiva acted as a "Vaidya" (doctor), the temple came to be known as Baidyanath. The temple complex consists of the main shrine and 21 other temples. During the holy month of Shravan, lakhs of pilgrims carry holy water from the Ganga River and walk barefoot to offer it to Lord Shiva. Baidyanath Dham is not only a religious center but also an important cultural landmark of Jharkhand. The architecture, spiritual atmosphere, and historic importance make it one of the most visited places in the state.

Parasnath Hill

Location

Parasnath Hill is located in Giridih district of Jharkhand, India.

About

Parasnath Hill, also known as Shikharji, is the highest mountain in Jharkhand with a height of about 1,365 meters. It is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites for followers of Jainism. The hill is believed to be the place where twenty out of twenty-four Jain Tirthankaras attained moksha (salvation). Every year, thousands of devotees visit this holy place to offer prayers and perform religious rituals. The journey to the top of the hill is both spiritual and adventurous, as pilgrims walk long distances surrounded by beautiful forests and peaceful natural scenery. Apart from its religious importance, Parasnath Hill is also known for its rich biodiversity. The surrounding forests are home to various plants, birds, and wildlife species. The peaceful atmosphere, fresh air, and scenic beauty make it a perfect destination for nature lovers and spiritual seekers. The sunrise view from the hilltop is breathtaking and attracts many tourists. Parasnath Hill represents faith, devotion, natural beauty, and cultural heritage of Jharkhand.

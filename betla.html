Betla National Park

Location

Betla National Park is located in Latehar district of Jharkhand, about 170 km from Ranchi.

About & History

Betla National Park is one of the first national parks in India to become a tiger reserve. The name BETLA stands for Bison, Elephant, Tiger, Leopard, and Axis (spotted deer), which represent the major wildlife species found in the park. Established in 1986 under Project Tiger, the park is part of the Palamau Tiger Reserve. It covers a large area of dense forests, grasslands, waterfalls, and hills. The region is rich in biodiversity and provides habitat to elephants, deer, monkeys, and many bird species. One of the main attractions of Betla National Park is the jungle safari. Visitors can explore the forest by jeep safari and experience wildlife in their natural environment. There are also watchtowers where tourists can observe animals safely. The park also contains historical ruins of old forts built by the Chero kings, adding historical importance to the natural beauty of the place. Betla National Park is a perfect destination for adventure lovers, wildlife photographers, and nature enthusiasts. It represents the rich ecological heritage of Jharkhand and attracts tourists throughout the year.
